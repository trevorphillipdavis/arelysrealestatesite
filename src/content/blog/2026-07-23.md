The South Florida residential real estate market continues to demonstrate notable activity across the region, with Broward County and Miami-Dade County leading recent reports. 

In Broward County, recent housing data highlights continued growth and steady market metrics. Total home sales in Broward climbed year-over-year for the fourth consecutive month, fueled by a 26% increase in single-family home transactions and a 15.5% rise in existing condo sales. High-end activity remains a primary driver, with sales priced at  million and above increasing by 35%. On the municipal front, Fort Lauderdale announced plans to keep its property tax rate flat for the 20th consecutive year while advancing structural and resilience investments, including a proposed  million City Hall project and .3 million allocated toward the Fortify Lauderdale flood mitigation program. 

Meanwhile, neighboring Miami-Dade County maintains strong regional momentum. Miami-Dade posted its strongest June in three years, with total home sales rising year-over-year for the tenth consecutive month. High-end single-family and condo transactions across the broader South Florida market continue to see positive year-over-year transaction growth without relying heavily on seller price cuts, supported by steady global demand and cash-buyer activity.

---

### My Take
Looking at the data, the narrative that South Florida real estate is slowing down doesn't hold up. Broward’s steady single-family demand paired with Fort Lauderdale's disciplined tax and infrastructure planning shows a market maturing into a stable groove rather than overheating. Buyers and sellers who focus on realistic pricing and solid fundamentals are finding plenty of room to transact successfully, proving that local real estate here remains resilient against broader economic static.
